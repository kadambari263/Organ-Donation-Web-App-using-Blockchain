What is organ donation ?

Organ donation is the act of allowing healthy organs or tissues from one body to be transplanted into another person who needs them to survive or regain normal function. It represents a powerful bridge between loss and life — where one individual’s organs can restore sight, breathe life into failing lungs, make a heartbeat possible again, or allow someone to live free from dialysis.

It is a medical marvel rooted in humanity — turning the end of one life into a new beginning for another. Organ donation stands as a silent promise that life can continue, even beyond death.

Who Can Donate?

  • Living Donors: Can donate a kidney, part of the liver, or bone marrow.
  • Deceased Donors: Organs are donated after brain death or cardiac death with prior consent or family approval.
  • Anyone can register, regardless of age, gender, or health status.

  1. Register on the official health portal in your country.
  2. Inform your family of your decision.
  3. Carry a donor card or have your status on your ID (where available).
